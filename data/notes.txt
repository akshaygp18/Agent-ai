In Canada, English and French are the official languages. Approximately 54% of Canadians have English as their mother tongue, while 19% have French. Other non-official languages spoken in Canada include Mandarin, Punjabi, Cantonese, Spanish, Arabic, Tagalog, Italian, German, and Tamil. The federal government practices official bilingualism, and Quebec has established French as the only official language of the province.
Christianity is the largest religion in Canada, with Roman Catholics representing 29.9 percent of the population, followed by Christians overall representing 53.3 percent. Other significant religions in Canada include Islam (4.9 percent), Hinduism (2.3 percent), Sikhism (2.1 percent), Buddhism (1.0 percent), Judaism (0.9 percent), and Indigenous spirituality (0.2 percent).
